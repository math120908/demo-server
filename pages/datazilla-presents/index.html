Context & Problem

"In January 2024, my team was told: build a data platform for LinkedIn's new foundation model. We had no spec, no staffing plan, and the model team couldn't tell us what they needed. The only thing clear: we were the blocker."

Scaling Data Volume

Decision 1: Daily Collection + Decoupled Joins

  • Day-1 decision: collect daily, not batch.
  • Tradeoff accepted: data duplication across days
  • Upside: preprocessing checkpoints + easily extensible
  • Adding a surface = adding one parallel job, no redesign needed

The Leadership Arc

Proposed daily-partitioned schema early → Rejected by model team → Tried 4 alternatives → Kept pushing → Eventually adopted

Scaling to More Customers

Decision 3: Configurable Workflow

Before

Each use case required a custom pipeline. Model team was the only customer. Adding a new consumer meant weeks of development.

After

JSON-driven configurable workflow. Config file defines activity filtering, feature selection, output schema. Same framework serves 360Brew, LMS AI, Retention AI, and inGen on a daily basis.

CK proposed the architecture. Xiaoyi Duan developed the implementation.

Decision Scaling Dimension Result
D1: Pipeline Architecture Data volume 1 → 13 surfaces
D2: Schema Evolution Data size per record Stable at 8.6T tokens
D3: Configurable Workflow Number of customers 1 → 4 teams daily

The Hard Part

Leadership Challenges

Partner Dynamics

  • Model team: credit asymmetry — model works = their success; doesn’t work = data’s fault
  • Delayed feedback — looked at our February data only in April
  • Published paper without including anyone from the data team

My response: Proactively sought 3 alternative customers (Retention AI, Ads, Jobs) despite leadership saying to “focus”

People

  • Team wanted ML work, not data engineering
  • Frustration with model team dynamics
  • Learned helplessness → 3 people left the team

My response: Gave ownership not just tasks. Coaching mindset. Listened, advised, stood with them. 1 teammate got promoted.

“The hardest part wasn’t scaling the data — it was keeping the team motivated when our customer treated us as a service, not a partner.”
